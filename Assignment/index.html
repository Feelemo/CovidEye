Advice for the Public

What is Considered as Being in Contact?

The World Health Organization (WHO) defines the characteristics of a contact as anyone with the following exposures to a COVID-19 case, from 2 days before to 14 days after the case's onset of illness:

  • Being within 1 metre of a COVID-19 case for >15 minutes;
  • Direct physical contact with a COVID-19 case;
  • Providing direct care for patients with COVID-19 disease without using proper personal protective equipment (PPE);
Thus, please be vigilant at all times and responsible. We urge you to be honest and transparent about any possible exposure of your friend/family member to the COVID-19 virus. You can reduce your chances of being infected or spreading COVID-19 by taking some simple precautions:

Sanitize your hands regularly

Regularly and thoroughly clean your hands with an alcohol-based hand rub or wash with soap and water that can kill viruses that may be on your hands.

Use face mask at all times

The general public is advised to wear a fabric mask if there is widespread community transmission, and especially where physical distancing cannot be maintained.

Seek medical assistance

If you have a fever, cough and difficulty breathing, seek medical attention, but call by telephone in advance if possible and follow the directions of your local health authority.
